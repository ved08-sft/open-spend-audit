# Performance Metrics & Instrumentation — SpendOptima

As a B2B lead-generation engine for Credex, SpendOptima's analytics are optimized around conversion velocity, capital efficiency, and viral loops rather than generic traffic vanity metrics (like daily active users).

---

## 1. The North Star Metric: Qualified Saved Dollar Value (QSDV)

### Definition:
**QSDV** is the *cumulative monthly savings surfaced for qualified startup audits (>=$100/mo savings) that successfully capture an email lead*.

$$\text{QSDV} = \sum (\text{Monthly Savings of Audits with } \ge \$100\text{ savings}) \times \text{Lead Capture Completed}$$

### Why this specific metric?
- **Alignment with Credex Value**: Credex monetizes when startups buy discounted bulk credits. A startup saving only $5/mo on a single Copilot seat has no incentive to buy credits. A startup leaking **$500/mo** is highly motivated.
- **Value-First Capture**: QSDV directly tracks *realized value*. It measures both the *depth* of the problems we are solving (dollars saved) and our *ability to capture interest* (lead captured). Improving QSDV directly increases Credex's sales pipeline quality.

---

## 2. Three Input Metrics Driving the North Star

To grow our North Star (QSDV), we track and optimize three core input drivers:

### Input Metric 1: Blended Audit Completion Volume (BACV)
- **Definition**: The total number of unique, fully populated audits run on the dashboard.
- **Impact**: Drives the top of our funnel. More completed audits increase the statistical probability of capturing high-value startups.

### Input Metric 2: Value-to-Lead Conversion Rate (VLCR)
- **Definition**: The percentage of completed audits that go on to submit the lead capture form.
- **Impact**: Measures our value hook. If this rate is low, it means users are looking at their savings and leaving without requesting the report. We target a **40% to 50% conversion rate** by offering highly valuable AI summaries and shareable URLs.

### Input Metric 3: Shared Viral Coefficient ($K_{\text{audit}}$)
- **Definition**: The average number of new completed audits generated by a shared dynamic URL (`/report/[id]`).
- **Impact**: Drives our zero-cost distribution loop. A higher viral coefficient reduces our organic customer acquisition cost to zero.

## 3. High-Signal Instrumentation Map
Before launching, we will instrument the application using lightweight tracking utilities (e.g. **PostHog** or **mixpanel**):

- **Event: `audit_tool_added`**: Tracks which SaaS tools are added most frequently. (Helps Credex know which credit pools are in highest demand).
- **Event: `audit_calculated`**: Tracks total calculated savings and overlap scores immediately upon mathematical resolution.
- **Event: `lead_submitted`**: Tracks form completion rates and optional field completions (role, company name).
- **Event: `report_copied`**: Tracks clicks on the copy-link buttons on shareable URLs, measuring viral loop intent.
- **Event: `consultation_click`**: Tracks clicks on Calendly integration links for audits showing >$500/mo savings.

---

## 4. Quantitative Pivot Trigger

### The Threshold:
Our critical pivot trigger is a **Value-to-Lead Conversion Rate (VLCR) below 15% after 300 unique completed audits**.

### The Action Plan:
If fewer than 15% of founders are willing to submit their email after seeing their calculated savings, it indicates that our value gate is weak. We would pivot our user acquisition strategy and product positioning:
1. **Friction Reduction**: We would test auto-generating a shorter 2-sentence preview of the AI Summary first, leaving the final paragraph gated.
2. **Format Pivot**: If email capture remains low, we will pivot SpendOptima from a general web app to a **browser extension** that automatically scans billing pages, shifting the value hook from manual inputs to automated background convenience.
